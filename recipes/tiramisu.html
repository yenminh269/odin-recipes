Tiramisu Recipes

Description

What is Tiramisu?

Tiramisu is a coffee-flavored dessert that features layers of homemade whipped cream, an egg yolk-enriched mascarpone filling, and coffee-soaked ladyfingers.

Tiramisu Origin

Tiramisu has Italian origins. The famous Italian restaurateur Ado Campeolis credited with its invention in the 1970s. In fact, he was widely known as "the father of tiramisu." The word "tiramisu" translates to "pick-me-up."

Steps to Make Tiramisu
    Ready to make the best tiramisu of your life?
  1. Make the Filling:

    2. Cook the egg yolks, sugar and milk until slightly thickened. Cook slightly, then chill in the fridge for about an hour. When the filling has fully chilled, mix in mascarpone cheese.

  2. Make the Whipped Cream:

    3. Beat heavy cream with vanilla extract until stiff peaks form

  3. Soak the Ladyfingers:

    4. Combine coffee and rum in a small bowl. Pour mixture overladyfinger that have been split in half lengthwise.

  4. Assemble the Tiramisu:

    5. Line the bottom of a baking dish with soaked ladyfingders. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers, then half of the whipped cream over that. Repeat in same order. Then dust with cocoa powder.
